  Mushroom Dryad's Saddle (Polyporus squamosus)


  ***Cerioporus squamosus*** aka *Polyporus squamosus* is a [basidiomycete](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basidiomycete "Basidiomycete") [bracket fungus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bracket_fungus "Bracket fungus"), with [common names](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_name "Common name") including **dryad's saddle** and **pheasant's back mushroom**.[\[2]](https://www.inaturalist.org/taxa/940028-Cerioporus-squamosus#cite_note-Spahr2009-2) It has a widespread distribution, being found in North America, Australia, Asia, and Europe, where it causes a [white rot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_rot "White rot") in the [heartwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heartwood "Heartwood") of living and dead [hardwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hardwood "Hardwood") trees. The name "dryad's saddle" refers to creatures in [Greek mythology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_mythology "Greek mythology") called [dryads](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dryad "Dryad") who could conceivably sit and rest on this mushroom, whereas the *pheasant's back* analogy derives from the pattern of colors on the bracket matching that of a [pheasant's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_pheasant "Common pheasant") back.
