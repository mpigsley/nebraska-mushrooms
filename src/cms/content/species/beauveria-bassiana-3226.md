The Icing Sugar Fungus is a widespread insect pathogen that can be found year-round infecting various species of hosts. The fungi erupt out of the joints of the insect exoskeleton as a white, powdery mass full of spores. The spores travel to other hosts and substrates via air currents.

![Joints](/img/beauveria-bassiana-3226-6.jpeg)

#### September 12th, 2023 Field Notes

- Growing on a winged insect on a rotting hardwood log suspended above a small creek in a low oak/hickory woodland area.
