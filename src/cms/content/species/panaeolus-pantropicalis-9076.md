The Pantropic Panaeolus is a dung decomposer that has a wide distribution across the world, though it has mostly been recorded in Florida. In Nebraska, it can be found fruiting on horse or bovine dung in the summer.

#### July 25th, 2023 Field Notes - Indian Cave State Park

- Growing in a small group on buried, old dung on a forest trail atop a ridge.
- Cuticle not gelatinous.
- The bottom half of the stipe is reddish.
- The cap possesses a small umbo.
