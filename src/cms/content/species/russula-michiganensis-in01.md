_Russula michiganensis_ is a mycorrhizal mushroom that can be found in the summer in Oak woodlands. This one was found in the Oak/Hickory woodlands of Indian Cave State Park in eastern Nebraska. It is an overall white-colored mushroom that stains black/gray where bruised.

The cap is evenly rounded and becomes deeply depressed with age. The gills are white, crowded, and broadly attached to the stem. The stem is sturdy. The taste is acrid. The spore print is white.
