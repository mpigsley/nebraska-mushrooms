The Little Nest Polypore (_Trametes conchifer_) is a common decomposer fallen woody debris that can be found from the spring through the fall. This fungus starts out looking like a small white cup growing out of fallen branches and twigs, and later extends to be more "shelf-like" with age, resembling Turkey Tail (_Trametes versicolor_) with concentric zones of dull colors. It can commonly be found growing on dead or dying Elm (_Ulmus sp._). The consistency is tough, and the spore print is white.
