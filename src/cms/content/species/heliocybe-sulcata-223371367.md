The Sunray Sawtooth (_Heliocybe sulcata_) is a widely distributed decomposer of wood (especially [quaking aspen](https://www.mushroomexpert.com/trees/populus_tremuloides.html)) that can be found from April to September.

The cap is distinctively pleated, with reddish scales in the center that form ridges on the top of the pleats toward the margin.

![Cap](/img/heliocybe-sulcata-223375670-2.jpeg)

The gills are white, serrated, and are attached to the stem or pulling away from it. It's not uncommon to find two caps fused at together towards the base of the stem.

![Hymenophore](/img/heliocybe-sulcata-223375670-1.jpeg)

The spore print is whitish.

![Spore print](/img/heliocybe-sulcata-223371367-6.jpeg)
